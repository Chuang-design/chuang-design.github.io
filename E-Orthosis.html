E-Orthosis

E-Orthosis explores augmenting off-the-shelf orthoses with embedded electronics to enable interactive, data-driven rehabilitation. This project combines fabrication, sensing, and wearable computing.

This project focuses on the integration of pressure sensors, IMUs, and BLE modules into existing orthotic braces, enabling fine-grained monitoring of joint movement and compliance tracking in rehabilitation.

We developed a series of prototypes and tested them with real users in clinical environments. Results show enhanced feedback and engagement compared to traditional rehabilitation routines.

Publication: ACM CHI 2023, Full Paper

Award: Best Paper Honorable Mention
